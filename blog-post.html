The Wonders of Iceland!

  • Collections
  • 12/1/2020
  • Shweta Loomba
  • (14) Comments

Iceland should definitely be a location on your bucket list! Each season has unique activities and sights to see. We went during the beginning of September and after landing at the Reykjavík airport, we rented a car which is probably the easiest and best way to get around the city. Be sure to pack cold and rain because the weather can be quite unpredictable.

After checking into our Airbnb we decided to do some grocery shopping and grab some lunch. We saw a Taco Bell so naturally that was our first choice, but it was quite a disappointment. It did not at all taste like the Taco Bell in the States. Once you reach Iceland you will also notice the peculiar smell of the water. This is because of the Sulfur, which is why when you turn on the hot water it tastes and smells like eggs. However, Iceland has some of the cleanest and freshest water! You just have to turn the tap to cold water and it will no longer taste or smell like eggs. So do not waste your money on buying water bottles.

The First Activity: The Blue Lagoon After recovering from some jet lag my friends and I headed over to The Blue Lagoon, a staple when visiting Reykjavík. We chose the comfort package which included a Silica Mud Mask and 1 free drink. Dipping into the steaming water of the Lagoon with the brisk air outside felt amazing. This whole experience was so relaxing and definitely worth it! And if you are looking for some insta-worthy pictures this would be your spot!

Golden Circle We had an early start to our day because we booked a tour that covered The Golden Circle, Thingvellir National Park, Snowmobiling on a Glacier and a Northern Lights Tour. This tour was nice because it covered everything in a single day! Although if you have a car rented it can be done on your own as well. The sights were absolutely beautiful but the highlight of the entire tour and the trip was Snowmobiling on a Glacier! It is the most exhilarating experience ever and I recommend it to anyone traveling to Iceland! Unfortunately, we didn’t get to see the Northern Lights since the weather was bad but that will give me an excuse to make another trip!
